    Welcome to Marine & Ports Services

    The Department's primary role is to oversee marine affairs in Bermuda. We are responsible for the safe movement of both international shipping and the operation of commercial and recreational boat traffic in local waters. The Department contributes significantly to the public transportation system through the operation of the Ferry Service, while also managing port operations including Pilotage, Tug and Line Boat assistance and maintenance of all marine Aids to Navigation.

    About Maritime Operations Centre

    The Maritime Operations Centre is the 24-hour operational hub of the Bermuda Government's maritime support program. Various distinct international safety and security responsibilities are overseen from a common facility where the use of specialist marine technology, training and experience all combine to create a high degree of situational awareness, communications connectivity and ultimately — timely decision making to support local and international operations.

    Rescue Coordination Centre (RCC Bermuda)

    Fulfilling international obligations to mariners and aviators requiring search and rescue support. RCC Bermuda coordinates SAR operations across a large area of the North Atlantic in accordance with international ICAO and IMO standards.

    Vessel Traffic Coastal Radar Surveillance (VTS)

    This serves as the backbone of the Bermuda Maritime Domain Awareness program and allows radar, AIS, radio direction finding and electronic chart technologies to be combined on one platform to assist border control, search and rescue, environmental protection and port operations.

    Coast Radio Station – Bermuda Radio (ZBR)

    This marine communications hub is interconnected with other Bermuda public safety agencies and fulfils regional communication obligations under the Global Maritime Distress and Safety System (GMDSS) where Bermuda has declared A1/A2 radio coverage in this area of the Atlantic. 24-hour watchkeeping on international distress frequencies and dissemination of Marine Safety Information — Notices to Mariners, Weather Forecasts and other SAR information — are broadcast around the clock.

    406 MHz Beacon Registry

    Management of the database holding details on beacons fitted aboard Bermuda vessels and aircraft required to fit COSPAS/SARSAT 406 MHz beacons. This is of direct benefit to RCC operations when an alert is detected and a response must be initiated based upon beacon user information on file.

    Long Range Identification and Tracking (LRIT)

    The global tracking of ships is an IMO requirement. Oversight of the Bermuda fleet sailing worldwide ensures that equipment faults aboard individual ships are quickly detected, port state compliance issues avoided, and remote monitoring assists in genuine emergencies.

    Seaport Security and the ISPS Code

    The Centre serves as the Government hub for Bermuda seaport security coordination. Mandatory filing with Bermuda Radio of pre-arrival information from all SOLAS vessels calling at Bermuda is required 48 hours prior to entry into territorial waters.

    Search and Rescue

    RCC Bermuda is responsible for coordinating Search and Rescue operations in a large area of the North Atlantic. The centre operates around the clock and maintains close liaison with the US Coast Guard, RCMP, and other international rescue coordination centres.

    In the event of a maritime or aeronautical emergency in the Bermuda Search and Rescue region, contact Bermuda Radio on VHF Channel 16 or telephone (441) 297-1010.

    Seaport Security

    Bermuda implements the International Ship and Port Facility Security (ISPS) Code at all commercial ports. Each port has an individual security plan and security guard services for cruise terminals is a Government contracted programme.

    All SOLAS vessels calling at Bermuda must file pre-arrival information with Bermuda Radio a minimum of 48 hours prior to entry into territorial waters.

    Security screening of all persons and goods embarking/loaded aboard ship is required under the ISPS Code. Port security certification and oversight is ultimately a UK Government function delegated to the Governor of Bermuda.

    VHF Radios / Licensing

    Marine VHF radios are essential safety equipment for any vessel operating in Bermudian waters. The use of marine VHF radios is regulated and operators must hold an appropriate licence.

    Key VHF Channels

    • Channel 16 — International distress, safety & calling channel. Always monitored by Bermuda Radio.
    • Channel 27 — Bermuda Radio working channel for routine traffic.
    • Channel 68/69 — Commonly used recreational channels.

    For information on obtaining a marine VHF licence, contact the Department at (441) 295-6575 or visit the office at East Broadway, Hamilton.

    Weather

    Bermuda Radio broadcasts regular marine weather forecasts on VHF Channel 27 in accordance with the GMDSS broadcast schedule. Forecasts include wind, sea state, visibility and any warnings.

    Sailing to Bermuda

    Bermuda is a popular waypoint for transatlantic voyages. All visiting yachts must comply with Bermuda Customs and Immigration requirements upon arrival.

    Arrival Procedure

    • Contact Bermuda Radio on VHF Channel 16 when within 50 miles of Bermuda.
    • Proceed directly to St. George's Harbour (primary Port of Entry for yachts).
    • Do not go ashore until cleared by Customs and Immigration.
    • Fly the yellow (Q) flag until clearance is granted.

    Contacts

    • Bermuda Radio: VHF Channel 16 / Tel: (441) 297-1010
    • St. George's Harbour Radio: VHF Channel 16
    • Bermuda Customs: (441) 293-2913

    Private Boat Registration

    The Boats and Moorings Section issues initial registrations and annual licensing (including transfer of ownership) for pleasure boats. There are approximately 7,000 private boats registered in Bermuda.

    Annual Licensing: All active vessels — punts, kayaks and boats — must be re-licensed annually between April 1st and May 31st of each year. "Active" means the boat is in the water or gets put in the water from time to time during the year.

    New Boat Registration

    After purchasing a brand new boat you are required by law to register it. Visit the Department at East Broadway, Hamilton with proof of purchase and the vessel details.

    Transfer of Ownership

    Whenever a vessel is sold, the new owner must attend the Department within 7 days to transfer title into their name.

    Calculating Fees

    When calculating boat fees you must use registered foot length as it appears on the Marine and Ports issued boat registration — drop inches, round down to feet only.

    Island (Charter) Boats

    The Code of Practice for the inspection and registration of all Island (charter) boats was introduced following consultation with local commercial boat operators. This Code firmly establishes basic safety and operational levels for the industry and ensures a continued improvement in the condition of boats that use Bermuda's local waters.

    The Code is continuously updated to reflect changing international standards. The most recent amendments include a section for operation and inspection of amphibious vehicles.

    📄 Code of Practice for Island Boats

    Getting a Mooring

    There are 2 ways to acquire a mooring: apply for a new one, or acquire an existing one. Space for new moorings is very limited, with most bays closed due to lack of space.

    Acquiring an Existing Mooring

    Acquiring an existing mooring means having a current mooring licensee sign their valid licence over to you to put in your name. The method for doing this is on the back of the mooring licence itself.

    Eligibility: To take a mooring licence you must have a boat registered in your name and cannot have other moorings in excess of boats owned. There is a $100 transfer fee. All transfers are subject to approval by the Department.

    New Mooring Application

    Before issuing an initial mooring licence, the Department inspects the site to ensure the proposed mooring poses no hazard to navigation or to other boats moored nearby.

    Wreck Removal

    The Boats and Moorings Section, in conjunction with the Aids to Navigation Section, assists with small boat wreck removal around the Island. Wrecks that pose a hazard to navigation are prioritised for removal.

    If you are the owner of a sunken or abandoned vessel, you are legally obligated to have it removed at your expense. Failure to do so may result in the Department removing the vessel and recovering costs from the registered owner.

    For queries regarding wreck removal, contact the Boats and Moorings Section at boatsandmoorings@gov.bm or call (441) 444-1221.

    Bermuda's waters offer a variety of recreational activities. The Department provides guidance and regulation of marine recreational activities to ensure the safety of all water users.

    Sport fishing regulations and licencing apply. Bermuda offers excellent deep water sport fishing.
    🏄
    Jet Skiing (PWC)
    Personal watercraft must be registered and operators must follow speed restrictions near shore.

    Shorelink Ferry Service

    Traveling by ferry in Bermuda is an excellent way to get around. The fast ferries are clean, spacious, air-conditioned and efficient. One of the highlights of traveling by ferry is the opportunity to see Bermuda's beauty from a different vantage point.

    Pilot Service

    This Section provides Branch Pilots to assist international shipping to safely navigate Bermuda's narrow channels. Transit of Bermuda's channels is limited to daylight hours only, however night pilotage does take place on a limited basis in cases where ships on passage may divert to Bermuda with medical cases onboard, or where ships alongside at the ESSO Oil Docks complete their cargo discharge and are able to shift to anchor in Murray's Anchorage.

    Aids to Navigation

    The section also assists with small boat wreck removal around the Island and with minor dredging works.

    Tug Service

    The Tug Service provides assistance to International Shipping and particularly to cruise ships and tankers in berthing, un-berthing and towing within local waters. It also provides limited towing and assistance to stranded vessels around Bermuda and offshore Rescue and Salvage.

    🛳
    Tug Powerful
    Large harbour tug with limited offshore capability. Can supply fuel, water and supplies. Advanced fire fighting and oil recovery capability.
    🛳
    Tug Faithful
    Large harbour tug with automatic towing winch and oil-dispersing booms. Can proceed to sea in favourable conditions.
    🛳
    Edward M. Stowe
    Medium tug operating as harbour tug with long range for SAR and ocean towing. Advanced fire-fighting capability.

    Employment

    Employment opportunities in tug service range from Seaman/Leading Seaman who handle lines on deck, and Tug Engineers assisted by Oilers who operate the mechanical and electrical plant. The Tug Pilot/Captain is in charge of the tug overall. Work hours are Monday through Friday 0830 to 1630 and may include weekends and overtime.
